Correlation ≠ Causation — But It’s Still Useful

We’ve all heard the phrase “correlation does not equal causation,” and while that’s true, correlation still has immense practical value. In exploratory data analysis, correlations can identify potential relationships worth investigating. For instance, if higher call volume correlates with slower response times, that doesn’t prove one causes the other — but it does point to an operational bottleneck worth digging into.

The key is to use correlation responsibly. It’s a signal, not a conclusion. Combine it with domain knowledge, hypothesis testing, and controlled experimentation. Correlation can’t give you causality, but it can absolutely help you find where to look next — and in data science, knowing where to look is half the battle.
