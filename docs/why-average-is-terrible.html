Why “Average” Is a Terrible Summary

“The average income is $75,000.” Sounds good, right? But what if half the population earns under $40,000 while a few outliers make millions? Suddenly that average doesn’t mean much. The mean can hide inequality and distort understanding — especially in skewed distributions. In many real-world datasets, the median tells a more truthful story about the “typical” experience.

As data scientists, it’s our job to question whether the summary statistics we report actually represent the story our data tells. Whenever possible, include context: show the spread, visualize the distribution, and mention outliers. “Average” is a starting point — not the conclusion. The best analysis moves beyond single numbers and captures the complexity of the underlying data.
